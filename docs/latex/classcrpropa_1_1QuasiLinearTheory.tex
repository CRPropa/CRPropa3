\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Scales the steplength according to quasi linear theory. 

Following quasi-\/linear theory \mbox{[}Schlickeiser1989\mbox{]}, the mean free path $\lambda$ of a particle with energy $E$ and charge $Z$ in a field with turbulence spectrum $\frac{k}{k_{\min}}^{-q}$ is \[ \lambda = {\left(\frac{B}{\delta B}\right)}^2 {\left(R_G\; k_{\min}\right)}^{1-q} R_G \equiv \lambda_0 {\left( \frac{E}{1 EeV}\frac{1}{Z} \right)}^{2-q} \] where $R_G = \frac{E}{B Z}$ is the gyro-\/radius of the particles. This class implements the rigidity dependent scaling factor used to modify the base step length. \begin{DoxyParagraph}{}
{\bfseries{}}\mbox{[}Schlickeiser1989\mbox{]} R. Schlickeiser, Cosmic-\/\+Ray Transport and Acceleration. II. Cosmic Rays in Moving Cold Media with Application to Diffusive Shock Wave Acceleration, The Astrophysical Journal 336 (1989) 264. doi\+:10.\+1086/167010. 
\end{DoxyParagraph}
